Render, Don’t Decode:
Weight-Space World Models with Latent Structural Disentanglement

Roussel Desmond Nzoyem1    Mauro Comi2
1 Centre for AI Fundamentals, University of Manchester  |  2 Interactive AI CDT, University of Bristol

Abstract

Training world models on vast quantities of unlabelled videos is a critical step toward fully autonomous intelligence. We introduce NOVA, a world modelling framework that represents the system state as the weights and biases of an auxiliary coordinate-based implicit neural representation (INR). This structured representation is analytically rendered, eliminating the decoder bottleneck while conferring compactness, portability, and zero-shot super-resolution. NOVA naturally disentangles structural scene components — background, foreground, and inter-frame motion — enabling users to edit content or dynamics without compromising the other. We validate our framework on challenging datasets, achieving strong controllable forecasting on a single consumer GPU.

Why rethinking world models?

  • Decoder bottleneck – Scalable video tokenisers allocate up to 87% of parameters to the decoder, limiting efficiency and interpretability.
  • Fixed resolution & rigidity – Changing output resolution requires expensive retraining or heuristic upsampling; no continuous rendering.
  • Non-portable latents – Abstract vectors are tied to a specific decoder, hindering reuse across tasks and models.
  • Poor disentanglement – Background, content, and motion remain entangled without costly auxiliary losses or adversarial training.
  • Weight-space instability – Prior weight-space world models suffer from numerical explosion (gradient / value clipping).

NOVA overcomes these issues via an analytical INR renderer, additive latent decomposition, and stable dynamics.

Method

NOVA evolves the hidden state directly in the weight space of an INR. The additive dynamics A(zt) + B(ut) induces disentanglement. The framework eliminates the traditional decoder: the continuous INR renders pixels analytically from latent weights, enabling parameter efficiency (≈40M total), portability, and zero-shot resolution flexibility. This weight-space world model compresses video dynamics into structured latent trajectories without a heavy spatial decoder — each frame is rendered by querying the INR at arbitrary coordinates.

Core Capabilities

✂️ Content / Action Editing (Zero-shot Disentanglement)

Additive decomposition zt (content) + ut (motion) lets users replace visual identity or steer dynamics independently. Unlike black-box latent models, NOVA provides structural intervention without auxiliary losses. Below: quantitative editing advantage over SOTA baselines (higher edit precision, lower content leak).

🔍 Zero-Shot Super-Resolution & Anti-Aliasing

Because the INR is a continuous function, NOVA can be rendered at any resolution without retraining. We apply a Nyquist frequency mask to Fourier features during inference, suppressing aliasing artefacts.
